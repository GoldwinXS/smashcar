A spatial-partition strategy game

WATERSHED

Springs (warm dots) each flow to the nearest well you place. A well can only carry so much flow — push too much through it and it floods, draining your reserve.

  • One move: drag wells around the field to re-divide it. The whole map re-slices to whatever is nearest — so every well you move changes everyone's boundaries.
  • Adapt vs. commit: moving a well unsettles it — its capacity drops, then recharges over a few turns while it sits still.
  • Plan ahead: each turn the springs grow and new ones bloom. Next turn's growth is shown as faint ghosts before you commit — arrange for what's coming.
  • Survive: keep wells from flooding. Demand never stops rising; last as long as you can.

Why this should stay interesting: the best layout is a nearest-point partition that's genuinely hard to optimise and shifts every turn as demand drifts — so there's no single trick that wins, only ever-better reading of the board. (Design grounded in published work on game "depth": Lantz et al. 2017; Nealen et al. 2011.)
