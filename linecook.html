LINE COOK

You and a rival AI cat draw counters on a shared kitchen grid. Close all four sides of a station and you claim its dish — and you cook again. Most dishes when the grid is full wins. The Capybara of Indifference judges, unmoved.

  • Draw a counter: click an empty edge between two dots.
  • Claim & repeat: completing a station's 4th wall claims it and gives you another move — so claims can chain.
  • The trap: drawing a station's third wall hands your rival a free dish. When you must, give away the smallest run — and the expert move is to sacrifice two to force your rival to open the next big chain.

Core = combinatorial game theory (the chain-and-parity math of Dots & Boxes, per Berlekamp). The greedy "grab every dish" move is provably a trap, and it's adversarial — so there's no single trick to solve it, only deeper parity play.
